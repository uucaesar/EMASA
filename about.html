Our Story

Why We Started

At EMASA (Eben Maths and Science Academy), we noticed a common challenge in the South African education system: in large, fast-paced classrooms, too many capable learners were falling behind in core STEM subjects.

Changing the Narrative

We constantly heard learners say, "I'm just not a Maths person," or "Physics is too hard." We founded EMASA to change that. Whether navigating the jump from Grade 9 to Grade 10 subject choices, or facing the high-pressure run-up to Matric Finals, learners need more than just textbook reading—they need guidance.

From Vision to Reality

What started as a vision to provide focused, high-quality extra lessons has grown into a dedicated academy that helps learners turn academic anxiety into academic excellence, unlocking the doors to their future careers.

Our Mission

To provide top-tier, personalised extra tuition in Mathematics and Physical Sciences that bridges the gap between the classroom and true understanding. We equip learners with the knowledge, exam techniques, and confidence to master the CAPS and IEB curriculums.

Our Vision

To be South Africa’s most trusted educational support academy, recognised for transforming learners' relationships with Maths and Science and helping them secure the university entrance requirements they need to pursue their dreams.

Teaching Philosophy

The EMASA Edge

We know that no subject is inherently "too difficult" when taught correctly. Our approach moves away from the traditional, overcrowded classroom dynamic.

1. Understanding Over Memorisation

Rote learning won't help with tricky Geometry or abstract Chemistry. We focus on the "why" and "how" behind theories, ensuring learners build a rock-solid foundation.

2. Exam Readiness & Technique

Knowing the work is only half the battle. We extensively use past NSC and IEB papers, teaching learners how to interpret questions and manage their time for maximum marks.

3. Confidence & Mentorship

Academic success is tied to self-belief. We celebrate small victories and encourage questions, helping learners tackle tests without fear in a supportive environment.
